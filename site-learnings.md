# What competitor sites do well — and what we should steal or counter

## Things worth copying / adapting

### From premium labs (Framebridge, Artifact Uprising, Minted)
- **Material spec sheets with photos.** Framebridge shows the actual frame samples zoomed in. Artifact Uprising shows the canvas weave and wood grain. We currently describe materials in text — adding macro shots of canvas weave / acrylic edge / metal finish would massively boost premium perception.
- **"Designed in [city]" provenance.** Artifact Uprising leans on "Denver, CO" hard. We should put a city/state on our Studio block — readers trust geography over abstract claims.
- **Real customer photos in lifestyle settings.** Both lean heavily on UGC of prints in real homes. We don't have these yet, but as soon as we ship to founding customers we should request photos and offer a 10% credit for them.
- **Frame finder / room visualizer.** Framebridge's "see it in your space" tool is a conversion driver. A simple "drop your generated art into a sample living room" mockup would close hesitant buyers.

### From premium catalogs (Saatchi, iCanvas)
- **Filter by room / mood / color.** Their browse pages are organized by "art for the bedroom" / "calming" / "blue-and-white" rather than just by size or material. We could mirror this on our gallery page to help users land on a prompt direction.
- **Curator picks / editor's notes.** Saatchi has short curator blurbs next to featured pieces. A "Founder's pick this week" panel on our homepage would humanize the brand and add freshness.

### From Displate
- **Aggressive shipping speed callouts on every product card.** We mention "7–14 days" once on the homepage. They put it on every SKU. Repeat it on the catalog cards.
- **Edition numbering as a marketing asset.** They number metal posters when they're limited. We can do this on signed founder editions: "Edition 1 of 50, signed by Lawrence."

### From POD (Printful, Society6) — counter-position, don't copy
- They lead with "starting at $14.99" and a giant catalog. We should do the **opposite**: lead with craft, not catalog size. Emphasize that we're selective.
- They use stock photos of the same canvas with different art swapped in. **Don't do this.** Show the actual production process — human hands stretching canvas, the signature being applied, the COA being numbered.

### From AI-direct competitors (NightCafe, Pictorem, Fy!)
- **Pictorem's AI generator UI is buried** under their main lab navigation. Ours is front-and-center on the homepage — keep it that way. This is a real advantage.
- **NightCafe's community gallery** drives huge engagement. We're not a community platform, but a "recently shipped" wall (with customer permission) would create the same social proof effect.
- **Fy! discloses AI-generated category prominently.** Builds trust by being upfront. We should similarly never hide that the art is AI-assisted — lean into it as the value, not the dirty secret.

## Conversion tactics worth borrowing

1. **Trust-bar above the fold.** Most premium labs have a strip showing "★★★★★ 4.8 from 12,000 reviews | Free shipping | 100% guarantee." Once we have real reviews, this is high-ROI.
2. **Sticky "ships in X days" countdown.** Several POD sites show "Order in the next 4 hours to ship by Friday." Creates urgency. Could be added to our checkout once production capacity is predictable.
3. **Material price comparison toggle.** Let users see the same generated art priced across canvas / metal / acrylic side by side without leaving the create flow. Pictorem does this poorly; we could do it well.
4. **Gift mode at checkout.** Framebridge and Minted both have a "this is a gift" flag that adds a gift note + hides the price on the packing slip. Easy win for holiday season.
5. **Save/wishlist a generation.** Users will generate things they love but aren't ready to buy. A "save to my gallery" button (already implied in our credit system) needs to be visually loud, not buried.

## Things competitors get WRONG that we should avoid

- **Pictorem's site is dense and lab-jargon-heavy.** Reads like a printing trade publication. Our copy is already friendlier — keep it that way.
- **NightCafe buries the print option** behind generation and credits. Hard to even find canvas. We surface "preview → order" as one continuous flow. Don't break this.
- **Saatchi requires login to see most prices.** Friction kills conversion. Keep our pricing transparent on every product card.
- **Society6/Redbubble drown the user in 10 million SKUs.** Our curated catalog (45 products) is a feature, not a limitation. Frame it that way: "We picked the sizes and finishes that actually look great. No infinite scroll of mediocrity."
- **Most AI-print sites use AI-generated marketing imagery on their own marketing pages.** Feels uncanny and undermines the "we care about quality" message. Use real photos of real product on our marketing pages — even if the art on the canvas was AI-generated.

## Top three changes I'd make on the site this week

1. **Add a Founder block** on the homepage (with Lawrence's photo + 1 sentence about the print-lab background). This is the single most differentiated thing we have. Ship it before any other UX polish.
2. **Add macro material photos** to the Materials section — canvas weave, acrylic edge depth, metal finish. Dramatically boosts premium perception.
3. **Number the COA** ("Edition 1 of N, signed and authenticated by Lawrence [Lastname]") — turns a generic certificate into a collectible artifact. Costs nothing to implement.
