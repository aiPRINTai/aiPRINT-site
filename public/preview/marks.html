The left panel simulates the CoA document layout. The S3 mark appears twice: top-left as the wordmark lockup, and bottom-right as the edition stamp. Reading across the sheet, the S3 mark functions as both masthead and seal — doing real work, not decoration.
Why not italic here? Playfair italic is already doing work on the body copy ("Mossy Pines No. 7" etc.). The mark needs to anchor the sheet visually, not harmonize into it. Roman lowercase ai = anchor.
System summary — G10 (italic, deep frame, glowing letters) owns digital surfaces: tab, header, iOS, OG. S3 (roman, rounded frame) owns archival surfaces: CoA, stationery, physical prints. Both stay in the indigo/violet palette so the existing OG, step photos, and wordmark don't need to change. If you want a single unified mark instead of a two-mark system, G10 can do both — but S3 reads better at CoA-size.
