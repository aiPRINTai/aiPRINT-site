Competitive position — one paragraph

The direct AI-print space is mostly commodity print-on-demand (Pixels, Redbubble, Sensaria) or app-attached generators (WOMBO, PrintMyAi). Nobody is positioning premium fine-art with COA + numbered editions + founder inspection. That's the moat — it's not features, it's positioning. The actual playbook is to steal gifting flow and risk-reversal language from custom pet-portrait sites, and room visualization + bundle mechanics from premium home-décor sites.

Top 15 tactics to implement

Ranked by impact-per-effort. Effort: S ≤30 min code · M few hours · L multi-day.

SHIP THIS WEEK Items 1–5 · ~2 hours total

Quick wins — copy + small UI changes
01."Is this a gift?" checkbox + free gift messageS

Source: Etsy / Framebridge / Apple. Most $95+ acrylics are gifts. Capturing intent unlocks gift-wrap upsell, suppresses price on packing slip, and segments your email list. Pure upside, near-zero cost.

02."Approval Guarantee" copy reframing on PDP + checkoutS

Source: Crown & Paw, Pop Your Pup. You already have watermarked previews — relabel the flow as "Love-It Guarantee: we don't print until you approve." Removes the #1 objection to AI prints (uncanny output) without changing a line of code.

03."Buy a 2nd print, 25% off" toast at checkoutS

Source: Desenio, Posterstore, West & Willow. Your generation already produces variants. Direct AOV lift via a checkout-stage offer.

04.Founder name + signature on the COA + confirmation emailS

Source: Allbirds, Patagonia. Doubles perceived value of the existing COA and justifies the $345–$995 price ceiling vs. commodity AI-print sites.

05.Brass plate / back-of-canvas card upsell ($5–10)S

Source: Framebridge "Make It Special". Pure margin upsell, attaches to the gift flow above. Reinforces premium positioning.

SOFT-LAUNCH WEEK Items 6–10 · 1–3 hours each

Meaningful additions you'll want before scaling spend
06.Multi-product preview: canvas vs. metal vs. acrylic side-by-sideM

Source: Pixels.com. Drives material upsell. Acrylic at $145 vs. canvas at $45 is your highest-margin variable; right now the customer picks one and never sees the alternatives.

07.Trending Prompts rail on homepageM

Source: Redbubble, Midjourney community. Solves cold-start ("what do I prompt?") for new users — biggest funnel drop-off in text-to-image commerce. Doubles as social proof.

08.Style-quiz onboarding → 5 personalized prompt suggestionsM

Source: Saatchi Art Advisory. Reuses your 258-prompt library tagged to style preferences. Massively reduces prompt anxiety for non-art-savvy gifters (60%+ of premium-print buyers).

09.Gift cards ($25 / $50 / $100, emailed, 1-year expiry)M

Source: Crown & Paw, Juniper Print Shop. Hits the "I want to give AI art but don't want to pick the prompt" buyer — currently completely uncovered by your funnel.

10.Credit-loop loyalty: $1 spent on a print = 1 generation creditS

Source: Sephora Beauty Insider. Extends your existing credit system into a retention engine. Trains buyers to come back without discounting prints.

POST-LAUNCH Items 11–14 · after first ~50 orders

Build when you have CAC + AOV data to compare against
11.Diptych / triptych checkout option (1 prompt → 3 variants → sold as a set)M

Source: West & Willow "Two Pet". AOV multiplier without new fulfillment.

12.Monthly Prompt Challenge with a $345 acrylic prizeM

Source: Minted, PrintMyAi. Free UGC + SEO content + email-list build. Cheaper than equivalent ad spend.

13.Concierge tier ($75): a real human refines your prompt over emailM

Source: Framebridge, Saatchi Curator's Circle. Captures corporate / anniversary / high-end gifters intimidated by prompt-writing. Pilot via email-only before building UI.

14.Quarterly designer / influencer "curated collection"M

Source: Juniper Print Shop. Credibility loan from someone who already has an audience.

BIGGER BETS Item 15 · multi-day projects

Defer until items 1–14 are shipped and the data tells you to
15.AR "View on My Wall" (mobile web)L

Source: IKEA Place, Wayfair, Society6. The single highest-converting feature in wall décor — but genuinely hard to build well. Defer until the shorter items are shipped and you've got conversion data to compare.
